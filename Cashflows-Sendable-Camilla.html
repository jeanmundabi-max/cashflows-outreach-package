The Vertical Gap Costing Merchant Pipeline

Cashflows has the products, the leadership, and the PE backing. SplitPay 20 to 30% uplift. Get Found grew 1,194% in 18 months. Markerstudy calls the service "flexible and dedicated." But every vertical gets the same pitch — and the merchants who need you most will not find you.

The Core Leak

Cashflows serves insurance brokers, dental practices, e-commerce brands, BNPL platforms, and property investors. Those results are real. But they sit in case study pages, disconnected from any vertical campaign.

An insurance broker searching "payment processing for insurance brokers" will find Stripe and Worldpay. Not Cashflows. Because Cashflows has one website, one pitch, and one "get a quote" form for every vertical. The products are strong — fast onboarding (80% of applications within 24 hours), Anytime Settlement, Interchange Plus Plus pricing. Merchants who find Cashflows tend to stay. The problem is not enough merchants are finding you.

The GTM Manager job description asks one person to build "frameworks and processes for all GTM programmes" across multiple verticals. That is 3 to 6 months of infrastructure before the first vertical launches.

No independent UK acquirer has built vertical-specific go-to-market. Stripe is horizontal. Worldpay is horizontal. Dojo is horizontal. The first acquirer to say "we are the payment partner for insurance brokers" owns that position. Cashflows has the case studies. Every quarter without vertical positioning is a quarter where generic competitors win by default.
